What does this receipt mean?

Every verification generates a cryptographic receipt — a tamper-evident record proving exactly what was checked, against which source, and when.

claim_idClaim ID
Plain English

A unique fingerprint for this verification event. Like a receipt number — no two are ever the same.

Why Regulators Care

If an examiner asks "did you screen this customer on this date?" — you produce the Claim ID. It's your proof of due diligence, retrievable at any time.

verified_atVerified At
Plain English

The exact UTC timestamp the live government source was queried. Not cached — when this specific check ran.

Why Regulators Care

BSA/AML examiners require proof of when a check occurred. A timestamp tied to a live source is legally defensible. A log entry from memory is not.

sources_checkedSource Checked
Plain English

Which government list was queried. OFAC SDN is US Treasury. BIS DPL is Dept. of Commerce. Each is an independent authoritative source.

Why Regulators Care

OFAC violations carry penalties up to $1M per transaction. Proving you checked the correct authoritative source — not an aggregator — is the difference between a clean audit and an enforcement action.

source_urlSource URL
Plain English

A direct link to the government database checked. Anyone — including your examiner — can visit this URL and verify the source themselves.

Why Regulators Care

Full provenance means the audit trail is independently verifiable. You're not asking an examiner to trust your records — you're showing them the government source directly.

match_scoreMatch Score
Plain English

100% = exact match. 85–99% = close but not identical — catches misspellings, alternate transliterations, and name variations.

Why Regulators Care

Sanctions evaders use name variations. Fuzzy matching with a documented confidence score demonstrates reasonable procedures defense under OFAC guidelines.

Inner-Circle provides custody of verification records, not legal compliance advice. Each receipt documents what was checked and when — your compliance team determines the appropriate action. OFAC requires recordkeeping for 5 years under 31 CFR Part 501.
