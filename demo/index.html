Every verification Inner-Circle performs generates a cryptographic receipt — a tamper-evident record that proves exactly what was checked, against which source, and when. Here is what each field means in plain English and why it matters to regulators.

claim_id Claim ID
Plain English

A unique fingerprint for this specific verification event. Like a receipt number — no two are ever the same.

Why Regulators Care

If an examiner asks "did you screen this customer on this date?" — you produce the Claim ID. It is your proof of due diligence, retrievable at any time.

verified_at Verified At
Plain English

The exact UTC timestamp the live government source was queried. Not when the data was cached — when this specific check was run.

Why Regulators Care

OFAC enforcement guidelines and BSA/AML examiners require proof of when a check occurred. A timestamp tied to a live source query is legally defensible. A log entry from memory is not.

sources_checked Source Checked
Plain English

Which government list was queried. OFAC SDN is the US Treasury Specially Designated Nationals list. BIS DPL is the Dept of Commerce Denied Persons List. OFAC Consolidated covers additional sanctions programs.

Why Regulators Care

OFAC violations carry penalties up to $1M per transaction. Proving you checked the correct authoritative source — not a third-party aggregator — is the difference between a clean audit and an enforcement action.

source_url Source URL
Plain English

A direct link to the government database we checked against. Anyone — including your examiner — can visit this URL and verify the source themselves.

Why Regulators Care

Full provenance means the audit trail is independently verifiable. You are not asking an examiner to trust your records — you are showing them the government source directly.

match_score Match Score
Plain English

100% means exact match. 85-99% means a close but not identical name — the system catches misspellings, alternate transliterations, and name variations that exact-match systems miss entirely.

Why Regulators Care

Sanctions evaders routinely use name variations. Fuzzy matching with a documented confidence score demonstrates reasonable procedures defense under OFAC guidelines.

Inner-Circle provides custody of verification records, not legal compliance advice. Each receipt documents what was checked and when — your compliance team determines the appropriate action. OFAC regulations require recordkeeping for 5 years under 31 CFR Part 501.
